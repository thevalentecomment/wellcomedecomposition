Body decomposition
in 5 stages
Fresh 24- 72 hours after death
Autolysis: Once the human dies, blood flow has stops meaning no oxygen is able to get to all parts of the body. Subsequently, carbon dioxide levels in the blood increase and pH level starts to decrease.
Bloat 3 - 5 days after death
Putrefaction: A few days after death, the body starts to bloat. Waste begins to build up in parts of the body. The internal organs start to break down which results in a strong odour being produced and the body discolouring.
Clothes start to embed the skin due to the immense pressure building up in the body. Fluids are forced out of different orifices during bloating, such as the mouth, nose and rectum.
Active Decay 8 - 10 days after death
Black Putrefaction: At this stage, the body experiences the greatest mass loss because of maggots feeding and all bodily fluids starting to drain out. The body starts to collapse. A very strong odour is released. The skin turns black as the body decomposes.
Advance Decay 2 - 3 weeks after death
Butyric Fermentation: Insect activity is reduced during this stage. When the carcass is located on soil, the area surrounding it will show evidence of vegetation death.
Dry Remains 1 month after death
Skeletonization: The body is almost completely dry at this point. The skin starts to look like a leathery surface. There is a slow rate of decay occurring now. Vultures aid in the removal of flesh and soft tissue left behind. This may take up to a year. Once all soft tissue is removed, the body is skeletonised.
